Cupid and Psyche

Antonio Canova

Cupid and Sage (aka Eros and Sage) are a mythical story that appears in Adrieus's novel "Golden Rhyme" and appears in a digressive form. Apureus may use other early stories as a model of his story to add dramatic effects. Since then, it has been considered a fable, a myth and a fairy tale.

Artemis of Gabii

Praxiteles

Gabii, unearthed in Italy in 1792. The goddess of hunting in the Greek mythology, Artemis, corresponds to Diana in Roman mythology. It is generally believed that this is one of the three Greek sculptors of the Hellenistic era, the work of Praxiteles. This Roman replica was made in the era of the Roman Emperor Tiberius.

Mattei Athena

Cephisodotos and Euphranor

The Athena Mattei is exceptional in that it is a Roman replica of a Greek statue that has also survived. The figure is a copy of the Piraeus Athena, a fourth-century BC bronze statue, created by Cephisodotus or Euphranor, discovered in 1959. The erstwhile goddess of war - seen here in a more benevolent attitude - wears a helmet and, across her chest, bordered with snakes and adorned with the head of the gorgon Medusa.

Winged Victory of Samothrace

H.W. Janson

Winged Victory of Samothrace is a sculpture of the goddess of victory in Greek mythology. It was created in the 2nd century BC and has been exhibited in the prominent position of the Louvre since 1884. It is one of the most famous sculptures in the world.

Vénus de Milo

Alexandros of Antioch

The statue was created between 130 and 100 BC and represents the goddess of love and beauty in Greek mythology, Aphrodite (the goddess in Roman mythology is Venus). This marble carved statue is 202 cm high, slightly larger than the true size of the human body. Founded in 1820 in Milos, Greece (modern Greek called Milo), it is called Venus of Milo. Currently in the Louvre in Paris, France.
